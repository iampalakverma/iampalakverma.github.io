Building Three Financial Statements from Raw Data

Working from an unstructured dataset of roughly 500 entries, I built the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement from scratch — no template to start from. The hardest part wasn't the formulas; it was deciding how to structure the raw data before a single number could be entered. Getting the three statements to tie out (net income flowing correctly into retained earnings, the cash flow statement reconciling to the balance sheet cash balance) was the real test of whether the model actually worked, not just looked complete.

What Building a Power BI Dashboard Taught Me About Messy Data

During my summer internship at Delhi Press, I built a 5-page Power BI dashboard covering 274 retail outlet records across 6 states. The dataset came from field surveys, which meant inconsistent formatting, duplicate entries, and gaps I had to work around before any DAX measure could run correctly. The dashboard ended up supporting real decisions on subscription pricing and lead conversion — a good reminder that most of the value in analytics work happens before the first chart is built.
